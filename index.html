The Geographic New York Times

A visualization of the most commonly referenced countries in the New York Times in 2012. Countries that are darker have more articles per capita. Alternatively, you can click the "Lights" button to see individual points of interest.

Built with d3.js using Natural Earth mapping data. Article data was collected from the New York Times API using Python.

Personal Top 40

The last four years of my last .fm history represented in a streamgraph. The top 40 artists in my library are ordered from most to least plays, top to bottom. The vertical thickness of a stream represents the listening count for that artist for that month.

You may toggle sound by clicking the speaker button in the bottom left; when sound is on, months with strong associations with songs or artists will play a track. This was made for an autobiographical project for my Information Visualization and Design class.

Built with d3.js; inspired by Lee Byron's streamgraph. Last.fm data was collected from their API with Python.

Calvin vs. Garfield

Here I attempted to justify my comic strip snobbery by showing that Calvin and Hobbes is objectively more creative than Garfield.

I categorized a full transcript of ten years of Calvin and Hobbes and a three year transcript of Garfield according to a word categorization dictionary called the Regressive Imagery Dictionary. Results were interpreted based on a psychological study; these results actually suggest Garfield is slightly more creative than Calvin and Hobbes.

Gesture-based scrolling

An application for touch-free scrolling. Uses your webcam to detect hand motions, which in turn scrolls whatever application is focused by your cursor. Allows for simultaneous browsing and sandwich-eating.

Built with Gaby Moreno Cesar and Jocelin Lee for our final project in Python Programming.

datapp

A web app that acts as a launch page for all the online services you use. It tracks your browsing history based on a whitelist the user constructs (sorry, NSA) and provides insight to your browsing habits through a series of visualizations.

Built with a team of six for a web development independent study. My primary contributions were the on the node.js backend and Chrome extension for data collection.
