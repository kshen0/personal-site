Projects

The Geographic New York Times

A visualization of which geographic regions are most commonly written about by the New York Times. Countries that are darker have more articles per capita. Alternatively, you can click the "Lights" button to see individual points of interest.

Built with d3js, the New York Times API, and Natural Earth.

Personal Top 40

The last four years of my last .fm history represented in a streamgraph. The top 40 artists in my library are ordered from most to least listened-to, top to bottom. The vertical thickness of a stream represents the listening frequency for that artist for that month.

You may toggle sound by clicking the speaker button in the bottom left; when sound is on, months with strong associations to songs or artists will play a track. This was made for an autobiographical project for my Information Visualization and Design class.

Built with d3js and the last.fm API; inspired by Lee Byron's streamgraph.

Gesture-based scrolling

An application for touch-free scrolling. Uses your webcam to detect hand motions, which in turn scrolls whatever application is focused by your cursor. Allows for simultaneous browsing and sandwich-eating.

Built with Gaby Moreno Cesar and Jocelin Lee for our final project in Python Programming.

datapp

A web app that acts as a launch page for all the online services you use. It tracks your browsing history based on a whitelist the user constructs (sorry, NSA) and provides insight to your browsing habits through a series of visualizations.

Built with node.js and a Chrome extension for history tracking. Built with a team of six for a web development independent study.
